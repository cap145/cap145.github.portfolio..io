What IF Project

Tools/software used: Adobe Illustrator

Project Reflection:

In my creation of this project the most difficult part was the creative writing aspect. While I do have a creative brain, my imagination needs a kickstart to run with an idea. I appreciated the prompts that were given to help in the writing aspect. Over all I was really happy with how this project turned out. The tokens I made showcase the abilities I learned while using Adobe Illustrator. I did not have many technical issues in using the software as the lessons were really extensive and detailed. This is a project of imagination and creativity. Read along and enter my mythozoological world showcasing the folklore of Ireland.

Come and join them for a showcase of mystery and magic! Scientists and researchers across the world have come together with their findings of a host of species from Irish folk tales for their annual symposium. Including Leprechauns, banshees, merrows, pookas, and Oilliphéists. Across the emerald hills, fog undulates and curves around old oak trees and a moss-covered stone path leading to an archway. A brass plaque read: The 125th annual symposium of Mythozoological Discoveries. Beneath it an arrow pointing through the tunnel to the Great Hall. Lit up by green and gold flame candles, shining against the stone walls. When inside, there was an unusual energy, a mix of wonder, skepticism, and a scent of pipe smoke. Scholars and scientists from around the globe whispering in hushed tones, carrying notebooks and field kits. This evening was not an ordinary scholarly gathering; tonight, they would unveil proof of Ireland’s most shifty creatures of story, superstition, and song. Dr. Carlton O’Malley stood at the podium, his round spectacles glaring in the candlelight. “For centuries,” she began, “We’ve dismissed these tales as folklore. However, the evidence compiled by our world renowned scientists and scholars speaks the truth.” Uncovering the first exhibit: a glass case containing a tiny leather shoe, smaller than that of a thumb. Leaning in and holding their breath the audience could barely stay still. “Recovered in a forest near Kerry County, residue analysis shows traces of gold dust and aged whiskey.” A ripple of awe ran through the crowd. Next came the haunting wail from a recording, the cry of a banshee. The sound filled the hall, cold and misery, raising goosebumps even on the most skeptical researchers. “Captured near an abandoned manor,” explained O'Malley in a hushed voice. “Notice the harmonic resonance unlike that of the human vocal frequency.” Then came footage of a merrow emerging from a moonlit covered ocean, her hair slicked back with seawater, eyes ancient and eerie. Samples of woven seaweed clothing were passed around the audience. The murmurs from the crowd were unmistakable. Was it possible? The presentation climaxed with chaos, the great screen showed sonar scans of something vast beneath Lake Fruiensholl, scales like armor, and eyes like fire, the legendary Oilliphést. Applause and disbelief erupted from the crowd. The air went stale. The creatures themselves were listening. Somewhere. Dr. O’Malley smiled faintly, closing his notes. “Ladies and gentlemen,” he said, “perhaps the greatest discovery is not that these beings exist, but that they never truly left us.” As the candles flickered, one could almost swear they saw movement in the shadows, a glint of gold, a ripple of seagreen, a flick of a tail; reminders that mystery and magic still linger in the air, just at the edge of science and magic.
